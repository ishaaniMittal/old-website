Nag Tibba Trek

3,022 metres (9,915 ft)

March 2014

It is the highest peak in the lesser Himalayan Region of Uttrakhand. It is believed to be the abode of 'Nag Devta' or God of Snakes, from which it borrows the first part of its name; 'Tibba' is a local word for hill or peak. Local villagers come here to offer their prayers to Nag Devta for the protection of their cattle.
